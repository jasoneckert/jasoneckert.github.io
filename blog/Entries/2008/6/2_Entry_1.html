Media trends

Monday, June 2, 2008

 

We often take the speed of technological innovation for granted today - the picture above is of the following media types from the late 1970s to present.


From left to right:

512KB 8-inch floppy (late 1970s to early 1980s)

1.2MB 5.25-inch floppy (early 1980s to early 1990s)

1.44MB 3.5-inch floppy (mid 1980s to mid 2000s)

1GB standard-size USB flash drive (early to mid 2000s)

4GB mini USB flash drive (mid 2000s to present)

8GB microSD flash card (present)


I graphed the size against the median of the time period that it was used - you will notice that storage capacity of removable rewritable media has been rapidly climbing in the past decade.  




Similarly, by graphing the size of each removable media (in inches) against the average time period it was used, you will also notice that media sizes are also reducing rapidly:
