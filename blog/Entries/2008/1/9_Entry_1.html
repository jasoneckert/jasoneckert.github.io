Getting ready for Linux...

Wednesday, January 9, 2008

 

Next week, I start teaching Linux again.  However, this time I am preparing an SGI Octane computer with a 64-bit R12000 MIPS CPU for it.


As an extra-curricular exercise, I am encouraging students to compile Gentoo Linux from scratch on this SGI computer.  It will involve netbooting a Gentoo image, manually creating filesystems, downloading files, compiling the kernel and all software customized for the particular platform.


Basically, installing Linux on a MIPS platform is somewhat like nailing jello to a tree, but highly educational - after compiling Gentoo for a specific MIPS hardware platform, it runs lightning fast.  I brought an SGI O2 in last year to demonstrate how a custom MIPS install on a 180MHz R5000 MIPS computer with 64MB of RAM will run Linux faster than our Core 2 Duo Dells with 2GB of RAM.  It was shocking.


And to make sure that it works, I just finished installing Gentoo on the Octane today and it is ten times better than the O2 :-)   Of course, I will be supplying a new hard drive that does not have Linux on it for the extra curricular project.


It really is too bad that SGI stopped supporting/developing the MIPS platform - it truly is a powerful platform (and the same one used in the Playstation 2).  However, Linux breathes new life into the MIPS platform today.
