Sci Fri talk at the Ontario Science Centre

Saturday, May 4, 2013

 

I was one of the panelists that talked at the Sci Fri event about Games & Art last night at the Ontario Science Centre.  I always like to make sure that any information I talk about is available afterwards to those who want it, so I’ve uploaded a YouTube video of my narrated presentation that you can watch here: http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JjC1gn20TR0&feature=player_embedded (turn the audio up, since I recorded this on a very noisy Alienware laptop ;-)


Sci Fri is a free event that is run each month on a Friday night at the Ontario Science Centre - there are tattoos (like the Pac-man one I got shown here), free food, panelist talks, a Q&A discussion, a battle of the sexes trivia, and a challenge. 


The panelist talks were all excellent - Jamie Woo (founder of Gamercamp) did a great opener encouraging the kids to make video games, Felan Parker talked about the defining features of video games as an art form, I did the talk shown above in my YouTube video link, and Tiffany Low Foon (one of our video game students) did a great talk on why video games are art and science.


For the challenge, they had Jeff the World (a prominent Toronto chiptune artist) help people make chiptune music using modded GameBoys and a GameCube as shown to the right (very cool indeed ;-)


Plus, kids got to check out the awesome Game On 2.0 exhibition!
