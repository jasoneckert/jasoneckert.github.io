The mythical Polybius arcade game

Thursday, May 23, 2013

 

There is an urban legend in game circles that started back in the arcade era.  It goes something like this:


A new Tempest-like arcade game called Polybius appeared in several suburbs of Portland, Oregon in 1981 and quickly became crazy popular.  However, the game had weird subliminal messages embedded in the graphics that caused unpleasant side effects in its players - amnesia, insomnia, nightmares, and suicide.  Men in black suits often watched players and collected data and eventually removed all traces of the machines.  The game was supposedly created by Ed Rotberg for a company called Sinnesloschen, and housed in a Pac-man style cabinet that was black.  The men in black were supposedly secret government agents.


So......is this a crude hoax or something with roots in reality?  It doesn’t take much effort to pick it apart.....


Ed Rotberg was the guy who created Atari Battlezone.  Battlezone which was such a good tank simulator, that the U.S. Military contracted Atari to build a version for their own simulator use.  Because of this well-known connection, Ed would be a somewhat believable part of a Polybius hoax.  The only problem?  Ed himself says that he has never been involved in such a game.


People during the video game craze of the 1980s were very worried about the addictive effects of video games - U.S. society agreed that they were warping our minds!  So why not make a hoax about a video game specifically designed to warp our minds?


Cathode ray tube (TV) displays have been known to cause epileptic seizures because electrons are “painted” across its surface at rapid intervals (this is why you see moving lines when you take a video of something on a TV).  Boom!  Seizures, nightmares, and other bad stuff that video games can do to add to the hoax.  And if you were to think of a video game in the 1980s that epileptics should avoid, it would probably be Tempest, with its incredibly cool and fast-moving vector graphics.  So why not make the Polybius game similar to Tempest in the hoax?


So.....on the surface, Polybius seems like a crude hoax created from convenient tidbits of information.  But why would people perpetuate this hoax?  Probably because it’s fun.....after all, people like government conspiracy theories.  That’s why the TV show “The X-Files” and movies such as “Men in Black” were so popular....we want to believe there is something spooky and full of conspiracy with the stuff we experience in everyday life.  It’s really no different than why we like to read fantasy novels, read comic books or watch movies.  


And since people like video games, creating a conspiracy/mystery surrounding a video game is exciting.  That’s essentially the plot of the movie “Cloak & Dagger” (1984).  And movies like “Tron” (1982), “War Games” (1983), “The Last Starfighter” (1984) and “The Lawnmower Man” (1992) are not too far off the mark.  Even “The Simpsons” payed homage to Polybius as shown below - take a careful note of the front of the arcade (Property of U.S. Government):
