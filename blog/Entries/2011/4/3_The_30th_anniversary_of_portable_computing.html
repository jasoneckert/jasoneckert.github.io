The 30th anniversary of portable computing

Sunday, April 3, 2011

 

The first portable computer, the Osborne 1, was released April 3rd 1981 - exactly 30 years ago today!


Although we think of portable today in the iPad sense, back in 1981, being able to actually carry a computer at all was a pretty amazing thing!  My Osborne 1 (shown above) weighs 24 pounds and can be carried by its handle when packed up.  You will notice that the keyboard doubles as a base, and attaches to the bottom with 4 heavy-duty clips for carrying.



To use it, you detached the keyboard, exposing the front panel (which contained the dual 5.25” floppy drives, ports, and 5” monitor), plugged the keyboard in and turned it on!


The Osborne 1 was finely crafted from heavy duty plastic and steel, which made it look superior to any computer of its day.  Because it was built like a tank, and had very good specs for the time (4MHz Z80 processor, 64K memory, parallel/modem/serial ports, CP/M OS with lots of software), and a relatively cheap price tag ($1795US), it sold very well.


I can definitely attest to the quality of the machine - my Osbone 1 still works today!


The picture to the right is the Osborne 1 boot (early BIOS) screen that prompts you to insert a floppy in the first disk drive (A drive) to load.  The writing is small (remember that it is a 5” display), but when you are using a command line system about 1-2 feet away from the computer, it really doesn’t matter.


Below is Microsoft BASIC loaded from one of those old disks.



The Osborne 1 was also the last Osborne computer made - after a year on the market, Adam Osborne let people know prematurely that he was developing a new Osborne model - so people waited for the new one instead of buying the Osborne 1 (and you can’t make new computers without constant cash flow!).


Regardless, what the Osborne 1 did was introduce portable computing - it was quickly followed by the Kaypro, Compaq and IBM portable computers.  But nothing was built quite like the Osborne 1 -  I used a Kaypro, Compaq, and IBM portable for a while, but they lacked the cool looks, ease of use, and build quality of the original portable computer!
