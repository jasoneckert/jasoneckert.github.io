Bullseye - A rare tabletop arcade from 1988 

Tuesday, May 10, 2016

 

I collect arcade games and other novelty items, so when I saw this little LED-based tabletop/bartop arcade machine from Merit Technologies (the same company that makes the wildly-famous Megatouch bartop arcade series) , I just had to pick it up ;-)




There is virtually no info about this game online except for a picture of it here http://www.arcade-history.com/?n=bullse ... l&id=14095

and here http://flyers.arcade-museum.com/?page=f ... 96&image=1


It has a pretty simple setup - behind the marquee is a single board powered by an Intel MCS-48 microprocessor (the same processor used to provide the sound on Donkey Kong).  This board also acts as the LED display panel:




If you remove the board, you’ll see the standard switching arcade power supply and roll-down quarter coin mechanism:




The best part of this game is the gameplay itself - it’s very simple, but very fun!  You get to choose 1 or 2 players, as well as one of two modes of play:

  1. •Compete for the highest score, or

  2. •Try to get 301 points exactly (if 2 player, the closest one to 301 points wins if no one gets 301 points exactly)


You get 5 rounds, with 3 darts per round.  Simply hold down the SHOOT button to start and release it at the right time to shoot your dart.  If you get a bullseye, then a cool sound plays.
