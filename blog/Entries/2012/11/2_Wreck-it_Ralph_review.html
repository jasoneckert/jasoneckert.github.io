Wreck-it Ralph review

Friday, November 2, 2012

 

The Disney movie Wreck-it Ralph premiered today in the movie theaters, and I just had to see it ;-)  Overall, the movie definitely lives up to the hype this past year.  Like all Disney animated movies, there is a plot with depth, a touching message, and plenty of humorous references.


The best part of the movie for me was the large number of subtle references to various aspects of the video game industry (old and new).  For example, the owner of the arcade (Litwak) wears a black-and-white striped referee shirt just like Walter Day (the founder of the famous Twin Galaxies organization that tracks arcade high scores (the character even looks like him too ;-). 


They also poke a lot of fun at the common features used within various video game genres.  For example, in Sugar Rush, they have a mini-game that allows you to build your car as well as leaderboards.  In Hero’s Duty (a Halo-like first-person shooter game), characters have high-definition graphics, and the game itself has a stereotypical armageddon-style theme with military-style violence, guided navigation, mission complete sequences, and cheesy backstory (which they tie in all throughout the movie very well).  And the Fix-it Felix game that contains Wreck-it Ralph is portrayed as a blocky 8-bit game from the early 1980s where characters only have one function and can only move in predefined ways.


I also like that they featured some cool “real” video games from the arcade era of the early 1980s.  The bar in the movie is actually the Tapper game (a real arcade game where you had to pour drinks for people).  The place where the bad guys meet for their focus group is actually in the inner ghost chamber of Pac-man, and the “homeless” characters in the game are from Q*bert (which fits because Q*bert fell out of style long ago).  There are also plenty of references to other games such as Mario, Burgertime, Paperboy, Joust, Frogger, Dig Dug, Qix, Sonic the Hedgehog, Mortal Kombat, Street Fighter, and more! 


Disney definitely didn’t skimp on portraying all of the different aspects of the arcade.  The side art on the arcade allows Ralph to unravel the mystery of Sugar Rush in the movie.  Plus, the movie talks about game glitches, the inner working of the game code, rebooting/resetting arcades to fix problems, and how the game characters die when the game is unplugged (which is true since old arcades are solid-state machines, and the characters loaded into memory are destroyed when the game is shut off). 


Although most references in the game were arcade-related, some were generic to video gaming.  For example, the lock to the code in Sugar Rush is actually a Nintendo NES controller, and there was a ton of modern player terminology (like on the sign in the picture at the top of this post: Will “non player character” in “frames per second” 4 food!). 


And I also have to mention the brief reference to Skrillex as the DJ at the party near the beginning of the movie ;-) 


But my favorite part is near the end, when Wreck-it Ralph says the gamers say we’re “retro”....which I think means old but cool.  And at the end of the credits, half of the screen fills up with 8-bit junk and the movie crashes, just like the famous “split-screen” bug in Pac-man when you get to the 256th level........very cool Disney.......very cool ;-)
