The Old triOS College Kitchener Campus

Wednesday, November 28, 2018

 

Back in 2010, the area around King & Victoria in Kitchener underwent a large transformation. As part of that transformation, the triOS College campus I worked at (shown in the 2009 Google image at the top of this post) moved from King & Victoria to King & Frederick (the big glass Market Square building).


But from 1998 to 2010, the old campus at King & Victoria was my second home and home to a lot of great memories. We had a massive common area with pool tables and arcade games. We stayed late to host LAN parties (um, I mean “network load testing”). The official campus pub was the Howl at the Moon Saloon a block away on King Street. We had a creepy basement dungeon that was used for PC repair, a huge instructor lounge, and a server closet that had a Buddha shrine in it (to keep things running smoothly, of course ;-)


The rooms were initially named after parts of the region (e.g. my classroom was the Berlin room). Below is a picture of my Exchange Server class watching me demonstrate something (I’m standing behind the monitors to the left of the picture), and the textbook in the foreground is the Microsoft Official Academic Curriculum (MOAC) I wrote for Wiley for this course (it’s nice to teach a course with a book that you wrote ;-)




And while we got a gorgeous new campus in 2010, I still miss the the old one.....well the memories at least.  Here’s what the campus looked like on the corner of King & Victoria before we moved (note the UHAUL sign to the right):
