Giving up Facebook - one month later...

Friday, November 2, 2018

 

Exactly one month ago, I removed my Facebook account after 10 years, including 500 friends and thousands of posts, pictures and comments. You can read why here.


It’s been an interesting month for sure! 

Long story short, I have no plans on ever returning to Facebook. But I’d like to use this post to detail what it’s like to give up Facebook for anyone thinking of doing it!


The first week is rough:

  1. •You instinctively go for your Facebook bookmark on your computer or look for your Facebook app several times a day until you realize that “Oh yeah - I quit Facebook.”

  2. •When you tell people that you deleted your Facebook account, most are horrified at the mere thought of the idea (which leads me to chuckle ;-)


The second week is where you are missed by others:

  1. •You occasionally crave a Facebook hit, but not nearly as much as during the first week

  2. •You end up checking out other social media more to make up for the void left by Facebook (LinkedIn, Twitter, Instagram, etc.)

  3. •A lot of people wonder what happened to you. Some of them contact you using some other means and ask you “if everything is alright” while others are a little less subtle and ask “did you unfriend me on Facebook?!?”


The third week is pretty awesome:

  1. •The cravings for Facebook disappear

  2. •Your mind is much clearer/focused - you’re not bombarded by skewed ads and posts that show other people’s biases, racism, negativity, etc.

  3. •You suddenly find you have more time (you still spend time on the Internet, but less time, and reading higher quality stuff)


And the fourth week? Well, that’s when you truly realize quitting Facebook was a great decision!
